WHAT IS THE COST OF LIVING CRISIS ?

The ‘cost of living crisis’ refers to the fall in ‘real’ disposable incomes (that is, adjusted for inflation and after taxes and benefits) that the UK has experienced since late 2021. It is being caused predominantly by high inflation outstripping wage and benefit increases and has been further exacerbated by recent tax increases.

BUDGETING

WHY IS IT IMPORTANT

The word budget to most of us means spend less but in its truest sense it means taking time to ensure you don't spend more than you have coming in and by budgeting you will have a financial plan in place to ensure this doesn't happen. Some signs you need to start budgeting could be:

  1. You can’t pay your bills
  2. Your credit cards are always maxed out
  3. Important bills are those you avoid
  4. Money is a consistent worry

Setting up a budget plan is reasonably straightforward but does involve being completely honest about your income and outgoings and then keeping it up to date as things change. Once you have compared your income and outgoings, if you are spending more than you have coming in you will need to decide what you can do without. On the other hand if you find you have more coming in than going out now is a good time to start saving for any unexpected costs. Budgeting is important because it helps you focus on what you spend your money on making it easier to prioritise your needs and which other things are most important to you.
